Spatially offset Raman spectroscopy (SORS) probes subsurface composition without sectioning tissue. Applied to bovine cartilage, this work establishes the first nondestructive method for mapping depth-dependent mechanical properties — Young's modulus and shear modulus — as a function of tissue depth and degradation state.

The technique could enable in vivo osteoarthritis staging without biopsy.

## Contributions

1. Designed SORS acquisition geometry and offset sequence for cartilage penetration depths.
2. Developed spectral decomposition pipeline to isolate depth-resolved Raman signals.
3. Correlated Raman signatures with mechanically measured elastic moduli across trypsin-treated samples.
